## Layer 5: Service-Level Messaging

### Tier 1 Diagnostics

#### Experiential Compliance Readiness Assessment
- **One-line:** We audit your experiential programme against six overlapping regulatory frameworks and tell you exactly where you are exposed.
- **Benefit:** Know your compliance position before your next activation — with a prioritised remediation plan, not a list of theoretical risks.
- **Before:** "We think we're compliant but nobody's actually checked across EU AI Act, EAA, DUAA, CSRD, DMCCA, and sector regulation simultaneously."
- **After:** "We have a documented compliance position, a risk register, and a 12-month remediation roadmap."
- **Proof point:** Covers six regulatory domains that no experiential agency currently addresses in combination. [Reference: Demand-Side White Space report — no agency has published a combined compliance framework]
- **Why Only Fisher XP:** Compliance consultancies don't understand experiential design. Experiential agencies don't understand regulatory frameworks. We operate at the intersection because our founders built premium brand experiences inside FCA-regulated businesses.

#### Experiential ROI Measurement Audit
- **One-line:** We assess how you currently measure experiential impact and redesign the framework to attribute pipeline, not count footfall.
- **Benefit:** Replace vanity metrics with attribution your CFO accepts. Protect the budget. Justify the programme.
- **Before:** "Our post-event report shows 2,000 attendees and a 4.2 NPS. Nobody believes this proves anything."
- **After:** "Our measurement framework attributed £1.2M pipeline to the Q3 conference within 10 working days."
- **Proof point:** 70% of event organisers report difficulty proving ROI (Bizzabo 2025). Fisher XP's framework is built into the experience design, not retrospectively applied.
- **Why Only Fisher XP:** We instrument measurement at concept stage — data capture is designed into the experience, not bolted on after.

#### Competitive Experience Benchmark
- **One-line:** We score your experiential programme against 5-8 competitors in your sector and show you where the white space is.
- **Benefit:** Brief your next activation from evidence, not assumptions.
- **Before:** "We attend competitors' events and form impressions."
- **After:** "We have a scored benchmark showing we lead on content but lag on data integration and accessibility."
- **Why Only Fisher XP:** Our methodology is evidence-based, structured, and repeatable — the same framework we used to build our own competitive intelligence.

### Tier 2 Strategic Engagements

#### Regulated Experience Design (Flagship)
- **Description:** End-to-end strategy, creative, and delivery management for brand experiences in regulated environments.
- **Benefit:** Experiences that are ambitious, memorable, and compliant. Concepts survive legal review first time because compliance is a creative input, not a review gate.
- **Before:** "Legal kills our best ideas three weeks before the event. We deliver safe, forgettable experiences because nobody will risk a compliance incident."
- **After:** "Our last activation was the most creatively ambitious we've ever produced. It passed compliance review in one round and attributed £800K pipeline within two weeks."
- **Proof point:** The Checkpoint identified that leading with creative ambition and closing with compliance credibility is the winning sequence. This service embodies that principle.
- **Why Only Fisher XP:** We don't design first and check compliance later. Compliance informs the creative brief from day one, which paradoxically produces bolder work — because constraints are resolved before ideation, not after.

#### Compliance-Integrated Event Strategy
- **Description:** A reusable framework for making your entire events programme compliance-ready without sacrificing creative ambition.
- **Benefit:** One framework your entire team and all agencies apply to every activation. Reduces legal review cycles by 50%+. Eliminates event-by-event compliance firefighting.
- **Before:** "We manage compliance event-by-event. Different agencies interpret requirements differently. Our legal team reviews everything manually."
- **After:** "We have a single compliance framework. Every activation runs through it. Legal review time halved. Consistency across all agencies."
- **Why Only Fisher XP:** We build frameworks, not just events. The framework becomes your organisation's operating standard — and it creates switching costs that protect the relationship.

#### Experiential Measurement Framework Build
- **Description:** Design and implementation of a measurement system connecting experiential activations to CRM pipeline and business outcomes.
- **Benefit:** Prove experiential ROI in language your CFO accepts. Justify the budget, protect the programme, demonstrate commercial value.
- **Before:** "We measure footfall, social impressions, and NPS. Nobody believes these prove anything. The CFO is questioning the entire budget."
- **After:** "We attribute £X pipeline to each activation within 10 working days. The CFO approved a 20% budget increase."
- **Why Only Fisher XP:** We integrate with your CRM and analytics stack from concept stage. The measurement architecture is designed into the experience, not applied retrospectively.

#### Brand Experience Redesign
- **Description:** Strategic redesign of an existing experiential programme that has become stale, formulaic, or out of alignment with brand positioning.
- **Benefit:** Transform a tired format into something distinctive — with the compliance and measurement infrastructure to sustain it.
- **Before:** "Our annual conference hasn't changed in five years. Attendance is declining. The board questions why we still do it."
- **After:** "We reimagined the format. Attendance up 30%. Pipeline attributed. Board approved three-year commitment."
- **Why Only Fisher XP:** We audit the existing programme against competitive benchmarks, audience expectations, and regulatory requirements before designing the new version. Most agencies skip the audit and jump to creative.

### Sentence Structure Preferences

- **Active voice.** "We design compliance into the creative brief" not "Compliance is designed into the creative brief by our team."
- **Short sentences.** Average 12-16 words. One idea per sentence.
- **Lead with the claim.** Opening line of any paragraph states the point. Evidence follows. Never open with setup or context.
- **Cite or qualify.** Every numeric claim has a source next to it. If unverified, flag it. Never present an estimate as a fact.
- **No exclamation marks.** Ever.
- **No rhetorical questions in written content.** Questions are for conversations, not documents.

| Term | Plain English | When to Use the Jargon |
|------|---------------|----------------------|
| EAA | European Accessibility Act — requires digital event elements to meet accessibility standards | Always spell out first, then abbreviate |

| CSRD | Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive — requires Scope 3 carbon disclosure including events | Spell out first; use "sustainability reporting requirements" in pitches |

### Register Guide: Same Idea Across Four Channels

**Core idea:** Fisher XP builds compliance into the creative process from day one.

**Thought leadership (LinkedIn article):**
"The experiential industry treats compliance as a review gate — something that happens after creative is developed. The predictable result: three weeks of legal revision cycles and a final product stripped of ambition. There is a better sequence. When regulatory requirements inform the creative brief before ideation begins, the constraints become structural inputs — and the creative output is paradoxically bolder."

**Proposal:**
"Fisher XP's methodology integrates compliance requirements into the creative briefing process at concept stage. This means every creative route presented to your team has been developed within the applicable regulatory framework (FCA, EAA, EU AI Act, DUAA). The practical benefit: reduced legal review cycles, faster approval, and creative concepts that do not require material revision for compliance reasons."

**Social media (LinkedIn post):**
"Most agencies design the experience, then send it to legal. Legal sends it back. Three weeks later, the concept is unrecognisable. We reverse the process. Compliance informs the creative brief before ideation begins. The result: bolder work that gets approved faster."

**Client delivery (email to client stakeholder):**
"As discussed, the creative routes we are presenting on Thursday have been developed against your FCA financial promotions requirements and EAA accessibility obligations. This means the concepts are compliant in principle — your legal review should focus on execution detail rather than fundamental direction. We expect this to reduce the approval cycle significantly."
