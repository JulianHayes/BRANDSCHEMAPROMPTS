## 1. Fee Structure and Project Types

### Tier 1: Diagnostics (Entry Point — Cash Generators)

| Service | Fee Range | Duration | Delivery Model | Margin |
|---------|-----------|----------|---------------|--------|
| Experiential Compliance Readiness Assessment | £5,000-£8,000 | 10 working days | Both founders, no subcontractors | 85-90% |
| Experiential ROI Measurement Audit | £6,000-£10,000 | 10-15 working days | Both founders + fractional data analyst | 75-80% |
| Competitive Experience Benchmarking Report | £3,000-£5,000 | 5-8 working days | One founder + desk research | 90% |

**Purpose:** Fast cash, low risk, demonstrates methodology, generates upsell pipeline. These bypass formal procurement at most firms (sub-£10K often approved by budget holder alone).

**Pricing rationale:** Benchmarked against management consultancy day rates for two senior consultants (£1,200-£1,800/day each). A 10-day diagnostic at £8,000 implies a blended day rate of £800 — deliberately below Big Four rates to reduce price objection, above freelance rates to signal quality. ### Tier 2: Strategic Engagements (Core Revenue)

| Service | Fee Range | Duration | Delivery Model | Margin |
|---------|-----------|----------|---------------|--------|
| Regulated Experience Design (full activation) | £50,000-£120,000 | 8-16 weeks | Both founders + production partners + specialists | 35-45% |
| Compliance-Integrated Event Strategy | £25,000-£45,000 | 4-8 weeks | Both founders, limited subcontracting | 60-70% |
| Experiential Measurement Framework Build | £20,000-£35,000 | 6-10 weeks | One founder + data/analytics partner | 55-65% |
| Brand Experience Audit + Redesign (for existing programmes) | £30,000-£50,000 | 6-12 weeks | Both founders + creative subcontractors | 50-60% |

**Purpose:** Core revenue driver. These are the engagements that build case studies, create switching costs, and justify the "Regulated Experience House" positioning.

**Pricing rationale:** Experiential agency fees for mid-market activations in the UK typically range from £40K-£200K for strategy-through-delivery. Fisher XP positions at the lower-to-mid range deliberately: high enough to signal premium capability, low enough that a two-person firm is credible. Agency fee benchmarks from Clutch, The Drum Agency Business, and IPA Agency Census suggest UK experiential agencies charge £150-£250/hour for senior strategic time and 15-25% management fees on production pass-through. [TRAINING DATA - VERIFY AGAINST CURRENT IPA/CLUTCH BENCHMARKS]

**Critical margin note:** The 35-45% margin on full activations assumes Fisher XP charges a strategy/management fee and passes production costs through at cost + 15-20% handling fee. If production costs overrun, they eat directly into margin. This is the single biggest financial risk in Tier 2.

### Tier 3: Retainers (Recurring Revenue)

| Service | Monthly Fee | Commitment | Delivery Model | Margin |
|---------|------------|------------|---------------|--------|
| Compliance Monitoring + Advisory | £2,000-£3,500/month | 6-month minimum | One founder, 2-3 days/month | 80-85% |
| Experiential Measurement-as-a-Service | £2,500-£4,000/month | 12-month minimum | One founder + analytics tools | 70-75% |
| Regulatory Watch + Impact Briefing | £1,500-£2,500/month | 6-month minimum | One founder, 1-2 days/month | 85-90% |

**Purpose:** Recurring revenue, switching costs, ongoing relationship. Retainers are the path from project-based to predictable income.

**Conversion assumptions:**
- Tier 1 to Tier 2 conversion: 30-40% (lower than Brand Schema's 60-70% assumption — experiential involves larger budgets and longer decision cycles)
- Tier 2 to Tier 3 conversion: 40-50% (clients who have seen a successful activation are more likely to retain for ongoing compliance/measurement)
- These assumptions are UNTESTED and must be validated against the first 5 Tier 1 engagements

## 3. Breakeven Calculations

### Breakeven 1: Survival (Cover All Costs at Minimum Draw)

**Annual cost at minimum draw:** £104,160

**Revenue needed (accounting for margin mix):**

| Revenue Source | Assumed Margin | Revenue Needed to Contribute £1 of Profit |
|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 Diagnostics (85% margin) | 85% | £1.18 |
| Tier 2 Activations (45% average margin) | 45% | £2.22 |
| Tier 3 Retainers (80% margin) | 80% | £1.25 |

**Scenario A: Diagnostic-heavy Year 1 (70% Tier 1, 20% Tier 2, 10% Tier 3)**
- Blended margin: (0.7 × 85%) + (0.2 × 45%) + (0.1 × 80%) = 59.5% + 9% + 8% = **76.5%**
- Revenue needed: £104,160 ÷ 0.765 = **£136,157**
- Equivalent to: ~20 diagnostics at £7K average

**Scenario B: Balanced mix (30% Tier 1, 50% Tier 2, 20% Tier 3)**
- Blended margin: (0.3 × 85%) + (0.5 × 45%) + (0.2 × 80%) = 25.5% + 22.5% + 16% = **64%**
- Revenue needed: £104,160 ÷ 0.64 = **£162,750**
- Equivalent to: 5 diagnostics (£35K) + 2 activations (£100K) + 1 retainer (£30K) = £165K

**Scenario C: Activation-heavy (10% Tier 1, 70% Tier 2, 20% Tier 3)**
- Blended margin: (0.1 × 85%) + (0.7 × 45%) + (0.2 × 80%) = 8.5% + 31.5% + 16% = **56%**
- Revenue needed: £104,160 ÷ 0.56 = **£186,000**
- Equivalent to: 2 diagnostics (£14K) + 2 activations (£140K) + 1 retainer (£30K) = £184K

## 4. Cash Flow Model — First 12 Months

**Assumptions:**
- Launch Month 1 (assume Q2 2026)
- Insurance and setup costs paid Month 1 (£15K upfront)
- First Tier 1 diagnostic sold Month 2, delivered Month 3, paid Month 5 (60-day terms)
- First Tier 2 engagement sold Month 4, delivered Months 5-8, invoiced Month 8, paid Month 10
- Subcontractor costs paid 30 days from their invoice
- Monthly burn £8,680

|-------|-----------------|---------------|-------------------------|-----------------|--------------------------|
| 1 | £0 | £0 | £0 | -£23,680 | -£23,680 |
| 2 | £0 | £0 | £0 | -£8,680 | -£32,360 |
| 3 | £7,000 | £0 | £0 | -£8,680 | -£41,040 |
| 4 | £0 | £0 | £0 | -£8,680 | -£49,720 |
| 5 | £7,000 | £7,000 | £0 | -£1,680 | -£51,400 |
| 6 | £0 | £0 | £8,000 | -£16,680 | -£68,080 |
| 7 | £0 | £7,000 | £12,000 | -£13,680 | -£81,760 |
| 8 | £80,000 | £0 | £15,000 | -£23,680 | -£105,440 |
| 9 | £7,000 | £0 | £5,000 | -£13,680 | -£119,120 |
| 10 | £0 | £80,000 | £0 | +£71,320 | -£47,800 |
| 11 | £3,000/mo retainer | £7,000 | £0 | -£1,680 | -£49,480 |

*Cumulative cash position assumes starting from £0 — actual position depends on founders' starting capital.

**Key insight:** The maximum cash gap is approximately **£119K** (Month 9) before the first major payment arrives in Month 10. This means:

- The founders need combined starting capital of at least **£60K-£80K** to survive to first major payment (assuming some overlap with freelance income)
- Or they need to structure payment terms differently: 30% deposit on signing, 30% at midpoint, 40% on completion — this would bring £24K forward to Month 5 and £24K to Month 6, dramatically improving cash position
- Tier 1 diagnostics are essential for cash flow even if margins are lower in absolute terms — they bridge the gap

## 5. Capacity Analysis

### Maximum Delivery Capacity (Two Founders)

**Available days per founder per year:** 220 working days - 25 holiday - 10 sick/contingency - 40 sales/admin = **145 billable days each = 290 combined**

**Capacity by tier:**

| Tier | Days per Engagement (Both Founders) | Max Engagements/Year | Max Revenue |
|------|-------------------------------------|---------------------|-------------|
| Tier 1 Diagnostic | 8-12 days (one founder) | 24-36 | £120K-£288K |
| Tier 2 Activation | 30-60 days (both founders) | 5-9 | £250K-£1,080K |
| Tier 3 Retainer | 2-4 days/month (one founder) | 8-12 concurrent | £144K-£480K |

**Realistic Year 1 mix (building pipeline):**

| Activity | Engagements | Revenue | Founder Days |
|----------|-------------|---------|-------------|
| Tier 1 Diagnostics | 8-12 | £40K-£96K | 80-120 |
| Tier 2 Activations | 2-3 | £100K-£270K | 80-150 |
| Tier 3 Retainers | 1-2 (from Month 6+) | £18K-£48K | 24-48 |
| **Total** | **11-17** | **£158K-£414K** | **184-318** |

This is consistent with the panel's Year 1 target of £250K-£400K but reveals that the upper end requires near-maximum utilisation — leaving little room for business development, methodology building, or the inevitable unpaid work (proposals, pitches, networking).

**A more realistic Year 1 target: £180K-£280K** — enough to clear survival breakeven but not reasonable-salary breakeven. Both founders will likely need to supplement with some freelance work in the first 6 months.

### First Hire Trigger

Hire when:
- Both founders are at >80% utilisation for 2 consecutive months AND
- Pipeline contains £100K+ of committed (signed or verbally confirmed) work AND
- Cash reserves exceed 4 months of burn including the new hire's cost

**Most likely first hire:** Production/project manager (£35K-£45K salary). Frees both founders from operational delivery and increases capacity for Tier 2 activations. Expected timing: Month 10-14 if Year 1 trajectory is on track.

## 6. Three-Year Client Lifetime Value

**Assumption:** A full-journey client enters via Tier 1, progresses to Tier 2, and retains on Tier 3.

| Year | Service | Revenue | Margin | Gross Profit |
|------|---------|---------|--------|-------------|
| Year 1 | Diagnostic (£7K) + Activation (£80K) | £87,000 | 52% | £45,240 |
| Year 2 | Second activation (£60K) + Retainer (£36K) | £96,000 | 58% | £55,680 |
| Year 3 | Retainer (£36K) + Ad hoc projects (£25K) | £61,000 | 72% | £43,920 |
| **Total** | | **£244,000** | **59%** | **£144,840** |

**Notes:**
- This is an optimistic full-journey scenario. Not every client will progress through all tiers.
- At 30-40% Tier 1→2 conversion and 40-50% Tier 2→3 conversion, approximately 15-20% of Tier 1 clients become full-journey clients.
- A client who only buys a Tier 1 diagnostic has a lifetime value of £7K. A client who buys one activation but does not retain has a lifetime value of £87K. The retainer is where compounding value lives.
