# Fisher XP — Kill Criteria Framework

Decision gates for Justin Fisher and Julian Hayes. Generated after reviewing all research outputs, Red Team critique, and Checkpoint review.

---

## Founder Context Update

Fisher XP is now a two-founder venture:

- **Justin Fisher** — Experiential delivery background. American Express premium programming (Savage Beauty, Platinum events), Aviva, Alter Domus. Creative execution and client relationship strength.
- **Julian Hayes** — Brand strategy and governance background. American Express global brand management (6+ years), Aviva wealth repositioning, Alter Domus verbal identity. Brand architecture, training, stakeholder management, AI integration.

The co-founder structure addresses the Red Team's single biggest concern (solo operator risk) and the panel's "founder dependency" flag. It also creates a natural division: Justin leads creative and delivery; Julian leads strategy, governance, and methodology.

However, the combined profile is strategy-and-brand heavy, production-light. Neither founder has run experiential production operations as a primary business. This means production partner relationships are not a nice-to-have — they are the structural foundation of delivery capability.

---

## Kill Criterion 1: Pipeline Velocity

**Measurable condition:** By the end of Month 3, Fisher XP must have a minimum of 5 active sales conversations (defined as: named buyer, identified budget, specific brief or need discussed, next step agreed) with a combined potential value of £150K+.

**Decision date:** End of Month 3.

**What happens if it fails:** If fewer than 3 active conversations exist by Month 3, the founders must immediately:
- Pivot to subcontracting for established agencies (Julian's brand governance and Justin's premium event experience are marketable to mid-size agencies as freelance capability)
- Reduce Fisher XP to a side venture pursued alongside paid freelance work
- Revisit positioning — "The Regulated Experience House" may not be generating meetings, in which case test "Premium Brand Experience with Built-In Measurement" or a simpler creative-led proposition

**How you will know it has failed:** Fewer than 5 names in a pipeline tracker with next steps and dates. If you cannot name 5 people you are actively talking to about paid work, you do not have a pipeline.

## Kill Criterion 2: First Paid Engagement

**Measurable condition:** Fisher XP must have at least one signed engagement (contract executed, deposit invoiced) worth £15K+ by the end of Month 5.

**Decision date:** End of Month 5.

**What happens if it fails:** If no signed engagement exists:
- Reduce scope to the productised diagnostic only (Experiential Compliance Readiness Assessment at £5K-£8K, fixed scope, 10 working days). This is faster to sell, easier to deliver, and generates case study material.
- If no diagnostic has sold either, both founders take freelance contracts to extend runway and treat Fisher XP as a 2-day-per-week build rather than a full-time venture.
- Do not invest further in infrastructure, tools, or marketing beyond what has already been committed.

**How you will know it has failed:** No signed SOW or contract in the deal folder. Verbal interest does not count. "Nearly there" does not count. A signature and an invoice are the only evidence.

---

## Kill Criterion 3: Delivery Without Incident

**Measurable condition:** The first two client engagements must be delivered on time, on budget, and without a material client complaint, production failure, or compliance incident.

**Decision date:** Completion of second engagement (likely Month 7-9).

**What happens if it fails:** If either of the first two engagements results in:
- A material budget overrun (>15% above quoted cost absorbed by Fisher XP)
- A client complaint escalated to senior stakeholder level
- A production failure (supplier no-show, technical failure, health and safety incident)
- A compliance gap identified by client legal or procurement post-delivery

Then: conduct a formal post-mortem within 5 working days. Determine whether the failure was:
- (a) A one-off operational issue (fixable — document lesson, adjust process, continue)
- (b) A structural capability gap (the founders cannot deliver what they are selling — pivot to consultancy/advisory only, drop production management from the proposition)
- (c) A partner failure (production subcontractor underperformed — replace partner, do not accept the same risk again)

**How you will know it has failed:** Client feedback score below 7/10 on a structured post-event survey. Budget variance report showing >15% overrun. Any written complaint from client.

---

## Kill Criterion 4: Measurement Methodology Proof Point

**Measurable condition:** By the end of Month 6, Fisher XP must have a named, documented measurement framework that has been applied to at least one engagement and produced a specific, quantified client outcome (e.g., "Event generated 47 qualified leads attributed to CRM within 10 working days" or "Compliance audit identified 3 regulatory gaps saving estimated £X in potential fines").

**Decision date:** End of Month 6.

**What happens if it fails:** If the founders cannot demonstrate a proprietary measurement output by Month 6:
- The "Regulated Experience House" positioning loses its key differentiator (the research and panel both identified measurement as the gap that separates Fisher XP from competitors)
- Revert to competing on creative quality and relationship — which is what every boutique does, and where the founders have no structural advantage over established players
- Consider whether the measurement capability requires a third person (fractional data analyst or measurement specialist) and whether the business can fund that hire

**How you will know it has failed:** No written methodology document. No client-facing measurement output. No quantified result to reference in the next pitch.

---

## Kill Criterion 5: Cash Runway Gate

**Measurable condition:** At no point in the first 12 months should the combined founders' cash reserve (business account + personal runway allocated to the venture) fall below 3 months of combined burn rate.

**Decision date:** Reviewed monthly. Trigger point: when reserves hit 4 months of burn (one month before the red line).

**What happens if it fails:** When reserves hit 4 months of burn:
- Both founders immediately seek freelance income to extend runway (Julian: brand strategy/governance consulting; Justin: experiential freelance production/creative direction)
- Fisher XP reduces to 50% of founder time until cash reserves recover above 6 months
- No new expenditure on tools, marketing, or infrastructure until cash position stabilises
- If reserves hit 2 months despite freelance income: wind down Fisher XP operations, fulfil existing commitments, and do not take new briefs

**How you will know it has failed:** Monthly cash position report (a spreadsheet, updated on the 1st of each month) showing reserves below the 4-month threshold. If neither founder is tracking this monthly, that is itself a failure — financial discipline is not optional.

## Post-Criteria Assessment

### Which criterion would actually cause the founders to pivot?

**Criterion 5 (Cash Runway)** is the only one that will force a decision regardless of optimism. Founders can rationalise a slow pipeline ("we just need one more month"), a late first engagement ("the brief is coming"), or a delivery issue ("we learned from it"). They cannot rationalise an empty bank account.

**Criterion 2 (First Paid Engagement)** is the second most likely to trigger action, because Month 5 with no revenue is psychologically difficult to sustain — especially with a co-founder, where each person's financial stress becomes visible to the other.

**Criteria 1, 3, and 4** are the ones founders typically push through even when they fail. "We'll get more pipeline next month." "The delivery issue was a one-off." "We'll build the methodology after the next engagement." These are the criteria that require the most discipline to enforce honestly.

### What is the single most dangerous assumption that has not been tested with real buyers?

**That financial services procurement teams will approve a vendor with zero years of trading history for experiential work in a regulated environment.**

The research establishes that the demand exists and the supply is inadequate. The founders' combined CVs (Amex, Aviva, Alter Domus) provide credibility. But credibility and procurement approval are different things. Many financial services firms have hard minimum requirements:

- Minimum 2-3 years trading history
- Minimum £500K-£1M annual revenue
- ISO 27001 or equivalent certification
- £5M-£10M professional indemnity insurance

A new company with two founders, zero revenue history, and no certifications may be filtered out before a human being reads the proposal — regardless of how strong the founders' personal credentials are.

**The workaround:** Target the first 2-3 engagements at firms where one of the founders has an existing personal relationship and can bypass formal procurement. Use these to build the trading history, case studies, and revenue record needed to pass procurement filters at firms where you have no relationship.

### At what monthly burn rate does the go/no-go decision need to be made, and by when?

**Estimated monthly burn (two founders):**

| Item | Monthly Cost |
|------|-------------|
| Julian — minimum salary/draw | £2,500 |
| Justin — minimum salary/draw | £2,500 |
| Insurance (PI + PL, annualised) | £800 |
| Software/tools | £300 |
| Accounting/legal | £200 |
| Travel/meetings | £400 |
| Contingency (10%) | £670 |
| **Total monthly burn** | **£7,370** |

At £7,370/month, the 3-month reserve minimum (Criterion 5) is £22,110.

**Go/no-go decision point:** If by Month 8, Fisher XP has not invoiced a cumulative £40K+ (enough to cover roughly 5 months of burn), the founders should have a formal conversation about whether to continue full-time or revert to freelance-funded part-time operation.

Month 8 is chosen because:
- It allows 3 months for pipeline building (Criterion 1)
- 2 months for closing a first engagement (Criterion 2)
- 3 months for delivery and invoicing
- Payment received approximately Month 8-9 if terms are 60 days

If the money has not arrived by Month 8, either the sales cycle is longer than modelled (and runway is being consumed faster than planned) or the market is not responding to the proposition.

---

*These criteria should be printed, signed by both founders, and revisited on the 1st of each month. The purpose of kill criteria is not to predict failure — it is to ensure that if failure is developing, the founders recognise it before it becomes irreversible.*
