### Revenue Scenario Grounding

B2B professional services benchmarks for new consultancies (IBISWorld, Management Consultancies Association):
- Average sales cycle for first engagement: 8-14 weeks [TRAINING DATA]
- Average conversion rate from proposal to signed: 20-30% [TRAINING DATA]
- Average time from first contact to proposal: 3-6 weeks
- Implication: outreach in Weeks 1-4 generates proposals in Weeks 5-9 and signatures in Weeks 8-13

Fisher XP's advantage: warm network (Amex, Aviva, Alter Domus contacts) should shorten the first 1-2 sales cycles. Cold outreach follows standard timelines.

## Success Metrics and Decision Framework

### Weekly Leading Indicators

| Indicator | Target | Red Flag |
|-----------|--------|----------|
| Outreach contacts made | 10+/week | Below 5/week for 2 consecutive weeks |
| Discovery meetings held | 2+/week | Zero meetings for 2 consecutive weeks |
| Proposals sent | 1+/week (from Week 4) | No proposals by end of Week 6 |
| LinkedIn engagement | 50+ interactions per article | Declining trend across 3 articles |
| Website enquiries | 1+/week (from Week 6) | Zero enquiries by Week 10 |

### Monthly Lagging Indicators

| Indicator | Month 1 Target | Month 2 Target | Month 3 Target |
|-----------|----------------|----------------|----------------|
| Revenue invoiced | £0 (realistic) | £0-£7K | £15K-£30K |
| Pipeline value | £80K+ | £120K+ | £150K+ |
| Signed engagements | 0 | 1 | 1-2 |
| Active retainer conversations | 0 | 0 | 1+ |
| Cash position vs. budget | Within 10% | Within 15% | Within 20% |

### Day 90 Decision Framework

**Scenario A: Proceed (Green)**
- 1+ signed engagement delivered or in delivery
- Pipeline above £100K
- Cash position above 4 months of burn
- At least 1 referral or inbound enquiry from non-network source
- **Action:** Continue full-time. Begin Q3-Q4 planning. Consider first hire timeline.

**Scenario B: Recalibrate (Amber)**
- 0-1 signed engagements, but pipeline above £60K
- Cash position above 3 months of burn
- Outreach generating meetings but not converting
- **Action:** Adjust pricing (reduce Tier 1 to £4K), adjust positioning (lead with measurement, not compliance), extend timeline to Month 6 checkpoint. Both founders maintain part-time freelance.

**Scenario C: Kill or Pivot (Red)**
- No signed engagement
- Pipeline below £40K
- Cash position below 3 months of burn
- Outreach not generating meetings
- **Action:** Stop full-time Fisher XP operation. Both founders return to freelance. Evaluate: (a) pivot to subcontracting model (Fisher XP as a freelance duo available to agencies), (b) pivot to pure consultancy (compliance advisory without experiential delivery), or (c) close.

## Go-to-Market Channels

### Channel 1: Personal Network (Weeks 1-4, then ongoing)

**Target:** 30+ warm contacts across Amex, Aviva, Alter Domus, and extended network
**Cadence:** 10 calls per week (5 each founder) for first 4 weeks
**Output:** Discovery meetings, referrals, introductions to decision-makers
**Owners:** Both founders, shared equally

### Channel 2: LinkedIn Thought Leadership (Weekly from Week 2)

**Target:** Build Fisher XP as a recognised voice on experiential compliance and measurement
**Cadence:** 1 article per week (alternating founders), 20 targeted connection requests per week
**Output:** Inbound enquiries, credibility, content for email campaigns
**Owner:** Julian (primary author), Justin (review and alternate posts)

### Channel 3: Targeted Email Campaigns (Bi-weekly from Week 4)

**Target:** 25 prospects per campaign, segmented by sector and role
**Cadence:** Every 2 weeks, 3-email sequence per prospect
**Output:** Discovery meeting bookings
**Owner:** Julian (drafts), Justin (personalisation for key prospects)

### Channel 4: Industry Events and Conferences (1-2 per month)

**Target:** Financial services and B2B marketing events
**Cadence:** 1-2 events per month, prioritising events where target buyers attend
**Output:** Face-to-face introductions, speaking opportunities (target: 1 speaking slot by Month 3)
**Owner:** Justin (attendance), Julian (speaking proposals)

### Channel 5: Agency Partnerships (From Week 5)

**Target:** 2-3 mid-size agencies who serve regulated clients but lack compliance capability
**Cadence:** 1 partnership conversation per week
**Output:** Subcontracting opportunities and referral agreements
**Owner:** Justin (agency relationships), Julian (partnership terms)
