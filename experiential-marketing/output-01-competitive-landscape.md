# 1. Scope Definitions

## Definition of Experiential Marketing

**Included: **Live brand experiences, immersive events, experiential activations, pop-ups, trade show design and activation, brand lands/permanent experiences, launch events with participatory elements, experiential retail environments.

**Excluded: **Pure media buying, traditional advertising, PR-only, social media management without a live component, conference/congress logistics-only (AV, catering, registration), pure digital marketing, influencer marketing without live activation.

## Operational Criteria

**Giant: **Annual revenue above GBP 20M (or equivalent), 200+ employees, multinational footprint or dominant single-market position, broad service offering including strategy through to production.

**Boutique: **Annual revenue below GBP 10M, under 50 employees, primarily UK-based, specialist or niche positioning.

Revenue figures for private agencies are estimates drawn from third-party databases (RocketReach, Growjo, LeadIQ, Owler). These frequently conflict. Where multiple sources exist, a range is provided with confidence flags. Holding company subsidiaries rarely disclose unit-level revenue. Exchange rates: USD 1 = GBP 0.79; HKD 1 = GBP 0.10; EUR 1 = GBP 0.86 (approximate, Feb 2026).

## Candidate Validation Notes

# 5. Methodology Notes and Limitations

**1. **Revenue data for private companies is unreliable. Third-party databases use algorithmic estimates that frequently conflict — sometimes by 2–3x. Only Pico (publicly listed) provides confirmed revenue data. All other agency revenue figures should be treated as directional estimates.

**2. **Legacy Drag and Resource Ceiling scores are evidence-based assessments, not objective measurements. Where evidence is thin (particularly AI adoption and compliance readiness), scores reflect the absence of evidence rather than confirmed capability gaps.

**3. **Agencies 16–30 in Table A have materially less evidence than agencies 1–15. Their inclusion ensures the brief’s requirement for 30 agencies is met, but scores should be verified through direct engagement or deeper research.

**4. **Boutique data is structurally harder to verify. Most UK boutiques do not file detailed accounts at Companies House or appear in third-party revenue databases. Scores for boutiques 6–12 are particularly uncertain.

**5. **Swerve was named in the brief as a starting boutique candidate. No UK-based experiential marketing agency by this name could be identified. Removed.

**6. **N2O was originally researched as a potential boutique but reclassified as a giant ($35M+ revenue, 450+ employees, acquired by Globe Groupe).

**7. **Holding company experiential divisions are included because they are materially active in experiential marketing, but unit-level financial data is typically not disclosed. Their Legacy Drag scores are structurally higher due to holding company overhead — these are features of the model, not anomalies.
