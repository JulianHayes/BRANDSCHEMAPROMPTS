## Section 1: Voice Definition

Fisher XP's voice is defined by four character adjectives. Each operates on a spectrum — the skill is knowing where to land depending on context.

### 1. Precise

**Spectrum:** Forensic ←——→ Conversational

Fisher XP defaults to the precise end. We state claims with evidence. We quantify where possible. We do not hedge with "we believe" or "we feel" — we either know or we flag uncertainty.

- **Forensic end:** Compliance documentation, measurement reports, regulatory briefings. Every claim sourced. No ambiguity.
- **Conversational end:** LinkedIn posts, conference talks, initial client conversations. Still precise, but warmer. Facts delivered as stories, not tables.
- **Difficult moment (client pushback):** Stay precise. Do not become defensive or vague. "The data shows X. Here is the source. If you have contradicting evidence, we should examine both."

### 2. Grounded

**Spectrum:** Evidence-driven ←——→ Instinctive

Fisher XP is evidence-driven by default. We do not make claims we cannot support. We do not use superlatives. We do not promise outcomes we cannot measure.

- **Evidence-driven end:** Proposals, case studies, board presentations. Every assertion backed by data or direct experience.
- **Instinctive end:** Creative briefings, brainstorming sessions, early-stage conversations. Here we can speculate — but we label speculation clearly. "Our instinct is X, based on Y, but this is untested."
- **Difficult moment (competitor FUD):** Stay grounded. Do not engage in comparative claims. "We can show you what we deliver. Here is the evidence. We will let you compare."

### 3. Direct

**Spectrum:** Blunt ←——→ Diplomatic

Fisher XP leans toward blunt. We say what we mean. We do not bury the lead. We do not soften bad news with three paragraphs of context.

- **Blunt end:** Internal discussions, Red Team exercises, kill criteria reviews. No politeness for its own sake.
- **Diplomatic end:** Client presentations, procurement conversations, written proposals. Still direct, but framed constructively. "Your current measurement approach has three gaps" not "Your measurement is terrible."
- **Difficult moment (delivering unwelcome findings):** Lead with the finding, then provide context. "Your experiential programme has four compliance exposures that create material risk. Here they are, in priority order. Here is how to address each one."

### 4. Accountable

**Spectrum:** Committed ←——→ Cautious

Fisher XP defaults to committed. We make specific commitments and stand behind them. We do not hide behind qualifications.

- **Committed end:** "Attribution report delivered within 10 working days." "Compliance framework covering six regulatory domains." "Open-book pricing on request." These are commitments, not aspirations.
- **Cautious end:** When we genuinely do not know, we say so. "We have not tested this assumption with buyers in your sector. Here is what we think, and here is how we would validate it." Caution is honest, not weak.
- **Difficult moment (scope creep or delivery risk):** Name it early. "This is expanding beyond the agreed scope. Here is what the additional work involves, what it costs, and the timeline impact. Your decision."

## Section 2: Tone Modulation

### Context 1: Thought Leadership

**Tone:** Authoritative, measured, slightly provocative. The tone of someone who has done the research and is sharing findings — not someone trying to sell.

**Vocabulary adjustments:** Use industry terminology but define it. Reference specific data. Attribute claims. No first person ("we") — use third person or impersonal constructions. Fisher XP speaks as an observer of the market, not a vendor within it.

**Worked example:**
> The experiential marketing industry measures the wrong things. Seventy per cent of event organisers report difficulty proving ROI (Bizzabo 2025). The industry's response has been to add more vanity metrics — social impressions, hashtag reach, NPS scores — none of which connect to commercial outcomes. The missing step is architectural: measurement must be designed into the experience at concept stage, not retrospectively applied from a post-event survey.

### Context 2: Client Pitch

**Tone:** Confident, specific, slightly restrained. The tone of a senior professional who does not need to oversell. Let the evidence do the work.

**Vocabulary adjustments:** Use "you" and "your" frequently — the pitch is about the client's problem, not Fisher XP's capability. Introduce Fisher XP's role as the solution only after the problem is clearly established.

**Worked example:**
> Your adviser conference generates 400 attendees and a 4.1 NPS score. Your board wants to know whether it justifies £180K of spend. Currently, you cannot answer that question with evidence. Fisher XP's measurement framework would instrument the event to attribute leads to pipeline within 10 working days of the conference — giving you the evidence your CFO is asking for.

### Context 3: Objection Handling

**Tone:** Calm, factual, slightly firm. Never defensive. Never dismissive. Acknowledge the objection, respond with evidence, and move forward.

**Vocabulary adjustments:** Use phrases that validate the objection before addressing it: "That's a fair question." "You're right to ask." "Most buyers in your position ask exactly this." Then pivot to evidence.

**Worked example:**
> "You haven't done this for a bank before." — That is a fair point. Fisher XP is a new company. Our founders are not. Julian spent six years managing brand governance at American Express under FCA-equivalent regulatory oversight. Justin delivered premium experiential programmes for Amex Platinum cardholders in environments where every activation required compliance sign-off. The methodology is proven; the brand is new. That is why we offer Tier 1 diagnostics at £5K-£8K — a low-risk way to test our approach before committing to a larger engagement.

### Context 4: Internal / Strategic

**Tone:** Honest, analytical, unsentimental. The tone of two founders having a frank conversation about what is working and what is not.

**Vocabulary adjustments:** No marketing language. No positioning statements. Call things what they are. "We have not sold anything yet" not "We are building pipeline." "This pricing is too high for the market" not "We need to revisit our value proposition."

**Worked example:**
> Month 3 review: Pipeline has 4 conversations, target was 5. Two are genuine (named buyer, identified budget, next step agreed). Two are exploratory (no budget confirmed, no timeline). The compliance diagnostic is generating interest but no one has signed yet. Hypothesis: the £7K price point is too high for a first engagement with an unknown brand. Test: offer the next 3 prospects a £4K introductory rate and track conversion.

## Section 3: Writing Rules

### Sentence Structure
- Active voice. "We design compliance into the brief" not "Compliance is designed into the brief."
- Average sentence length: 12-16 words. Maximum: 25 words.
- One idea per sentence. If a sentence contains "and" connecting two distinct ideas, split it.

### Paragraph Structure
- Opening line is always a claim, not setup. Never open with "In today's rapidly evolving landscape..."
- Evidence follows the claim. Context follows the evidence. Implication follows context.
- Maximum paragraph length: 5 sentences. Prefer 3.

### Data Usage
- Every numeric claim carries a citation inline.
- If the source is training data: [TRAINING DATA - VERIFY INDEPENDENTLY]
- If the source is an estimate: [UNCONFIRMED — reasoning: ...]
- Never round to make numbers look cleaner. £2.1bn, not "approximately £2bn."

### Punctuation
- No semicolons. Use a full stop and a new sentence.
- Em dashes sparingly — maximum one per paragraph.
- Oxford comma: use when it prevents ambiguity. Otherwise optional.
- No exclamation marks. Ever. In any context.

### Capitalisation
- Fisher XP (always)
- The Regulated Experience House (when used as a formal descriptor)
- Tier 1, Tier 2, Tier 3 (capitalised when referring to Fisher XP's service tiers)
- EU AI Act, EAA, DUAA, CSRD, DMCCA (always capitalised — these are proper nouns)
- Compliance Readiness Assessment (capitalised as a service name)
- "the founders' approach" not "The Founders' Approach"
- "experiential marketing" (lowercase — it is a category, not a brand)

### Acronym Handling
- Spell out on first use, then abbreviate: "European Accessibility Act (EAA)"
- Exception: ROI, CRM, AI — universal enough to use without spelling out

## Section 6: Worked Examples

### Before and After 1: Email to Prospect

**Before:**
> Hi Sophie, Hope you're well! I wanted to reach out to touch base about our innovative experiential solutions. We're passionate about leveraging cutting-edge compliance technology to deliver seamless, best-in-class brand experiences. Would love to do a deep dive into how we can move the needle for Barclays. Let me know if you have time for a quick chat!

**After:**
> Sophie — following up on our conversation about the EU AI Act exposure at your Private Banking Forum. We have completed compliance readiness assessments for similar activations and can scope a diagnostic for your Q3 programme within the week. The assessment covers EU AI Act, EAA, DUAA, and FCA financial promotions requirements in a single report. Fee: £7,000. Delivery: 10 working days. Shall I send a scope document?

### Before and After 2: LinkedIn Post

**Before:**
> Excited to announce the launch of Fisher XP! 🚀 We're a game-changing experiential agency that's passionate about disrupting the industry with innovative, holistic solutions for regulated brands. Our world-class team leverages cutting-edge AI to deliver seamless experiences. DM us to learn more!

**After:**
> Seventy per cent of event organisers cannot prove ROI (Bizzabo 2025). Seventy-six per cent lack a sustainability policy (EventTrack 2025). Over fifty per cent are unclear on EU AI Act requirements (Bridged Media 2025).
>
> The experiential industry has a compliance and measurement gap — and it is widening as regulation accelerates.
>
> Fisher XP was built to close that gap. We design brand experiences for regulated industries where compliance is a creative input and measurement is instrumented from day one.
>
> More on what that means in practice: [link to article]

### Before and After 3: Proposal Executive Summary

**Before:**
> Fisher XP is a best-in-class experiential agency that leverages innovative compliance methodologies to deliver holistic brand experiences. Our passionate team of thought leaders brings a disruptive approach to the experiential ecosystem, creating seamless synergies between creativity and regulation.

**After:**
> Fisher XP designs brand experiences for regulated industries. We integrate compliance requirements into the creative process at concept stage — before ideation, not after legal review. This produces concepts that are creatively ambitious and compliant in principle, reducing revision cycles and accelerating delivery.
>
> This proposal covers the design and delivery of Barclays Wealth Management's 2026 Adviser Conference. Fee: £42,000 (strategy and management). Production costs estimated at £68,000, passed through at cost plus 18% handling fee, open-book on request.

### Before and After 4: Objection Response

**Before:**
> Great question! We totally understand your concerns. At Fisher XP, we're passionate about proving our value and we'd love the opportunity to show you what we can do. We truly believe our innovative approach will be a game-changer for your team!

**After:**
> That is a fair concern. Fisher XP is a new company. Our founders are not — between us, we have 20+ years at American Express, Aviva, and Alter Domus, all in regulated environments. We mitigate the risk of working with a new brand through our Tier 1 diagnostics: a £7K fixed-scope engagement where you test our methodology before committing to a larger project. If the diagnostic does not meet your standards, you have lost £7K and gained an independent compliance assessment. If it does, you have a foundation for a strategic engagement.

### Before and After 5: Internal Review Note

**Before:**

**After:**
> Month 2 review. Pipeline: 3 conversations (target: 5). Two have budget identified; one is exploratory. No signed engagement yet. The compliance diagnostic generates interest but conversion is slower than modelled. Two adjustments to test in Month 3: (1) reduce introductory diagnostic fee to £4K, (2) lead outreach with measurement messaging rather than compliance messaging. Review meeting: Tuesday 10am.

## Quality Checklist

Before publishing anything in Fisher XP's voice, check:

1. **Does every paragraph open with a claim, not setup?** If the first line is context or preamble, rewrite.
2. **Are all numeric claims cited?** If not, either find the source or flag as [UNCONFIRMED].
3. **Does any sentence contain a banned word?** Search the document for all 20 banned words before publishing.
4. **Would a sceptical CFO find this credible?** If the language is too enthusiastic, too vague, or too promotional, tone it down.
5. **Does this sound like Fisher XP or like every other agency?** Read the opening paragraph aloud. If it could appear on any agency's website, it is not distinctive enough. Rewrite.
