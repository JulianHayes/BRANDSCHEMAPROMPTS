# Fisher XP — Service Architecture and Delivery Framework

Adapted from Brand Schema Prompt 5. Designed for a two-founder experiential marketing firm targeting regulated industries with "The Regulated Experience House" positioning.

## 2. Service Architecture — Three Tiers

### Tier 1: Diagnostics (Door Openers)

These generate cash quickly, demonstrate methodology, create upsell pipeline, and typically bypass formal procurement (sub-£10K budget holder approval).

#### 1.1 Experiential Compliance Readiness Assessment
- **One-line description:** Audit of your experiential programme against EU AI Act, EAA, DUAA, CSRD, and sector-specific regulations.
- **Benefit:** Know exactly where you are exposed before your next activation — with a prioritised remediation plan, not just a list of gaps.
- **Before:** "We think we're compliant but nobody's actually checked."
- **After:** "We have a documented compliance position and a remediation roadmap for the next 12 months."
- **Why Only Fisher XP:** Compliance consultancies don't understand experiential design. Experiential agencies don't understand regulatory frameworks. Fisher XP operates at the intersection.
- **Fee:** £5,000-£8,000
- **Duration:** 10 working days
- **Deliverable:** 20-30 page assessment with risk register, priority matrix, and 12-month action plan

#### 1.2 Experiential ROI Measurement Audit
- **One-line description:** Assessment of how you currently measure experiential impact, with a redesigned framework tied to business outcomes.
- **Benefit:** Replace vanity metrics with attribution that your CFO will accept.
- **Before:** "Our post-event reports show footfall and social impressions."
- **After:** "Our measurement framework attributes £X pipeline to each activation with 10-day turnaround."
- **Why Only Fisher XP:** We build measurement into the experience design, not retrofit it after the event.
- **Fee:** £6,000-£10,000
- **Duration:** 10-15 working days
- **Deliverable:** Current state assessment, redesigned measurement framework, KPI architecture, dashboard specification

#### 1.3 Competitive Experience Benchmark
- **One-line description:** Analysis of how your experiential programme compares to 5-8 competitors in your sector.
- **Benefit:** Know where you lead, where you lag, and where the white space is — before briefing your next activation.
- **Before:** "We attend competitors' events and form impressions."
- **After:** "We have a scored benchmark showing exactly where we differentiate and where we're behind."
- **Why Only Fisher XP:** Our competitive methodology is built on the same evidence-based framework we used to build our own business.
- **Fee:** £3,000-£5,000
- **Duration:** 5-8 working days
- **Deliverable:** Benchmark scorecard, competitive positioning map, opportunity brief

### Tier 2: Strategic Engagements (Core Revenue)

These are the engagements that build Fisher XP's reputation, generate case studies, and justify the premium positioning.

#### 2.1 Regulated Experience Design (Flagship Service)
- **One-line description:** End-to-end strategy, creative, and delivery management for brand experiences in regulated environments.
- **Benefit:** Experiences that are ambitious, memorable, and compliant — concepts that survive legal review first time because compliance is designed in, not added on.
- **Before:** "Legal kills our best ideas three weeks before the event. We deliver safe, forgettable experiences."
- **After:** "Our last activation was the most ambitious we've ever done and it passed compliance review in one round."
- **Why Only Fisher XP:** Compliance informs our creative briefing from day one. We don't design first and check compliance later.
- **Fee:** £50,000-£120,000
- **Duration:** 8-16 weeks
- **Deliverable:** Strategic brief, creative concept (2-3 routes), compliance sign-off pack, production management, on-site delivery, post-event attribution report within 10 working days
- **Delivery model:** Both founders (strategy + creative direction) + production partners (build, AV, technical) + specialists as required (data, accessibility, sustainability)

#### 2.2 Compliance-Integrated Event Strategy
- **One-line description:** Strategic framework for making your entire events programme compliance-ready without sacrificing creative ambition.
- **Benefit:** A reusable framework your internal team and agencies can apply to every activation — not a one-off audit.
- **Before:** "We manage compliance event-by-event, with different agencies interpreting requirements differently."
- **After:** "We have a single compliance framework that every activation runs through, reducing legal review time by 50%+."
- **Why Only Fisher XP:** We build frameworks, not just events. The framework becomes your organisation's operating standard.
- **Fee:** £25,000-£45,000
- **Duration:** 4-8 weeks
- **Deliverable:** Compliance framework document, decision trees for each regulation, template risk assessments, training session for internal team (2 hours), implementation support for first activation

#### 2.3 Experiential Measurement Framework Build
- **One-line description:** Design and implementation of a measurement system that connects experiential activations to CRM pipeline and business outcomes.
- **Benefit:** Prove experiential ROI in language your CFO accepts. Justify the budget, protect the programme, demonstrate value.
- **Before:** "We measure footfall, social impressions, and NPS. Nobody believes these prove anything."
- **After:** "We can attribute £X of pipeline to each activation within 10 working days."
- **Why Only Fisher XP:** Our measurement framework is built into the experience design — we instrument the experience from concept stage, not retrospectively.
- **Fee:** £20,000-£35,000
- **Duration:** 6-10 weeks
- **Deliverable:** Measurement methodology, KPI framework, dashboard design, data pipeline specification, first activation measurement report

#### 2.4 Brand Experience Redesign
- **One-line description:** Strategic redesign of an existing experiential programme that has become stale, formulaic, or out of alignment with brand positioning.
- **Benefit:** Transform a tired format into something distinctive — with the compliance and measurement infrastructure to sustain it.
- **Before:** "Our annual conference hasn't changed in five years. Attendance is declining. The board questions why we still do it."
- **After:** "We reimagined the format, attendance is up 30%, and we can prove the pipeline impact."
- **Why Only Fisher XP:** We audit the existing programme against competitive benchmarks, audience expectations, and regulatory requirements before designing the new version.
- **Fee:** £30,000-£50,000
- **Duration:** 6-12 weeks
- **Deliverable:** Programme audit, strategic redesign brief, creative concept (2-3 routes), implementation plan, measurement framework for the new format

### Tier 3: Retainers (Recurring Revenue)

Retainers create predictable income, deepen relationships, and build switching costs. They are the path from project-based to sustainable business.

#### 3.1 Compliance Monitoring + Advisory
- **One-line description:** Ongoing monitoring of regulatory changes affecting your experiential programme, with quarterly impact assessments and recommendations.
- **Benefit:** Never be surprised by a regulatory change. Receive clear guidance on what it means for your events programme before your legal team has to ask.
- **Fee:** £2,000-£3,500/month (6-month minimum)
- **Deliverable:** Monthly regulatory digest, quarterly impact assessment, ad hoc advisory (up to 2 days/month)

#### 3.2 Measurement-as-a-Service
- **One-line description:** Ongoing measurement and attribution for your experiential programme, delivered as a managed service.
- **Benefit:** Every activation measured to the same standard. Quarterly performance reviews. Annual programme ROI report for the board.
- **Fee:** £2,500-£4,000/month (12-month minimum)
- **Deliverable:** Per-event measurement reports (within 10 working days), quarterly programme reviews, annual ROI report, dashboard access

#### 3.3 Regulatory Watch + Brand Briefing
- **One-line description:** Monthly briefing on regulatory, competitive, and audience developments affecting experiential marketing in your sector.
- **Benefit:** Strategic intelligence that keeps your experiential programme ahead of regulatory and competitive shifts.
- **Fee:** £1,500-£2,500/month (6-month minimum)
- **Deliverable:** Monthly briefing document, one 60-minute call with senior team, annual landscape review

## 3. Client Journey: Tier 1 → Tier 2 → Tier 3

```
AWARENESS
  Thought leadership, conference speaking, warm introductions
  |
  v
TIER 1 DIAGNOSTIC (£3K-£10K)
  Low risk entry. Budget holder approval. 2-3 week delivery.
  Demonstrates methodology. Identifies specific gaps.
  |
  v
  Output: Gap report with recommendations that naturally lead to...
  |
  v
TIER 2 STRATEGIC ENGAGEMENT (£20K-£120K)
  Addresses gaps identified in Tier 1. Requires procurement.
  3-4 month delivery. Produces case study material.
  |
  v
  Output: Successful activation + measurement proof point
  |
  v
TIER 3 RETAINER (£18K-£48K/year)
  Ongoing relationship. Switching costs increase over time.
  Client becomes reference and referral source.
```

**Conversion triggers:**
- Tier 1 → Tier 2: The diagnostic reveals gaps that the client cannot fix alone. The Fisher XP team has demonstrated competence and trust during the diagnostic. Budget exists or can be allocated. (Target conversion: 30-40%)
- Tier 2 → Tier 3: The activation was successful. Measurement proved ROI. The client wants consistency. Internal team lacks capacity to maintain the framework without external support. (Target conversion: 40-50%)

**Critical design choice:** Every Tier 1 deliverable must include a "What This Means" section that maps directly to Tier 2 services. This is not upselling — it is showing the client the logical next step based on their own data.

### Production Partners (Must Be Confirmed Before Launch)

Fisher XP does not own production capability. All physical production is delivered through partners. This is a deliberate strategic choice — it keeps costs variable and overhead low — but it requires:

- **3-5 confirmed production partners** with agreed rate cards and SLAs
- At least one partner with experience in financial services events (knows the compliance environment)
- At least one partner with strong technical capability (AV, digital integration, data capture)
- Written agreements (not verbal) covering: rate card, response time, quality standards, insurance, data handling

**Non-negotiable:** Fisher XP must vet production partners on compliance readiness. A partner who cuts corners on data handling or accessibility destroys the positioning.

## 5. Pricing Principles

1. **Fixed fees for diagnostics, not day rates.** Clients buy outcomes, not time. A £7K diagnostic is easier to approve than "15 days at £500/day."

2. **Strategy fee + production pass-through for activations.** Fisher XP charges a fixed strategy/management fee (30-40% of total project value) and passes production costs through at cost + 15-20% handling fee. Open-book pricing on request — this is a differentiator against giants who obscure mark-ups.

3. **Monthly retainers with 6-month minimum.** Shorter commitments are not worth the sales effort. 12-month retainers get a 10% discount.

4. **Payment terms: 30/30/40 for Tier 2.** 30% on signing, 30% at midpoint, 40% on completion. This is non-negotiable for a startup — it addresses the cash flow gap identified in the unit economics. If a client insists on 60-day terms post-completion, price accordingly (add 5-8% to cover the financing cost).

5. **No speculative creative.** Fisher XP does not pitch creative concepts for free. Tier 1 diagnostics serve as the paid entry point. If a client wants to see creative thinking before committing to Tier 2, they buy a Tier 1 diagnostic first.

## 6. Three Decisions to Stress-Test

### Decision 1: Should the Competitive Experience Benchmark be paid or free?

**Case for free:** It generates leads. It demonstrates capability. It lowers the barrier to engagement. Many consultancies offer free benchmarks as a sales tool.

**Case for paid (recommended):** Free work devalues the methodology. It attracts tyre-kickers, not buyers. A £3K-£5K benchmark filters for clients who are serious about action, not just curious about data. It generates immediate cash. And it sets the precedent that Fisher XP's thinking has commercial value.

**Decision:** Paid, but offer a condensed "Executive Snapshot" (2-page summary, 1 hour of work) as a free lead magnet for warm prospects.

### Decision 2: Should measurement be a standalone service or bundled into every Tier 2?

**Case for standalone:** It is a distinct capability with its own value proposition. Some clients want measurement without activation design. Standalone pricing is clearer.

**Case for bundled (recommended):** Measurement is the differentiator. If it is optional, some clients will opt out to save money — and Fisher XP loses the proof point. Building measurement into every Tier 2 engagement means every project produces an ROI case study. The cost is absorbed into the overall fee.

**Decision:** Bundle a standard measurement framework into every Tier 2 engagement. Offer enhanced measurement (deeper attribution, custom dashboards, ongoing tracking) as a Tier 3 retainer upsell.

### Decision 3: Should Fisher XP accept projects outside regulated industries in Year 1?

**Case for accepting:** Revenue is revenue. A non-regulated activation still builds the portfolio, generates cash, and keeps the founders busy. Being too narrow risks starving the business.

**Case for declining (recommended with caveat):** Every non-regulated project dilutes the positioning. If Fisher XP's first three case studies include a music festival, a product launch, and an investor day, the positioning is incoherent. The market sees a generalist, not a specialist.

**Decision:** Accept non-regulated work only if: (a) it pays above £30K, (b) it does not require Fisher XP's name on public materials (white-label or subcontracted), or (c) the client is in a sector adjacent to regulated industries (e.g., professional services, insurance broking) where the work reinforces the positioning. Decline work that creates portfolio confusion.
