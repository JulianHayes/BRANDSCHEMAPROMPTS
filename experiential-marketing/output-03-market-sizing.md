# **1. Market Size Ranges for 2025–2026**

## **1a. Why Sources Differ: Scope Matters**

Market research firms use radically different definitions of ‘experiential marketing.’ This explains why published estimates range from $44 billion to $128 billion for the same year. The analysis below separates two definition clusters:

**Narrow definition (agency services): **Covers experiential marketing agency/service fees only — strategy, creative, production, activation. Excludes venue hire, catering, travel, media spend. This is the definition used by Business Research Insights, Cognitive Market Research, Congruence Market Insights, and EconMarket Research.

**Broad definition (total experiential spend): **Covers all spend associated with experiential marketing including venue, production, sponsorship activation, staffing, travel, media amplification. This is the definition used by PQ Media and the Business Visits & Events Partnership.

Both are legitimate. For the purposes of this analysis, we use the narrow (agency services) definition as our primary frame because it represents the addressable market for agencies — the spend that could switch provider.

## **1c. UK Experiential Marketing: Market Size Derivation**

No standalone, high-quality UK experiential marketing agency market report was found via open search. Three derivation methods are presented below.

### **Method 1: Direct country estimate (Cognitive Market Research)**

Cognitive Market Research reports UK experiential marketing service market at USD $2,499m (2024), growing at 3.3% CAGR. Extrapolating:

**2025: **$2,582m / £2,040m

**2026: **$2,667m / £2,107m

### **Method 2: UK share of global (Europe = 30%, UK = ~35% of Europe)**

Business Research Insights reports Europe holds approximately 30% of the global experiential marketing market. The UK is the largest European market for advertising (AA/WARC: total UK ad spend £46bn in 2025, roughly 35% of Western European total).

**Working: **$55.5bn × 30% (Europe) × 35% (UK share of Europe) = $5.8bn / £4.6bn

*This is higher than Method 1 because the UK over-indexes on marketing spend relative to GDP. It likely captures some broader spend beyond pure agency services.*

### **Method 3: Bottom-up from Gartner/IPA data**

Gartner 2025: Event marketing receives 19.3% of offline channel spend. Offline is ~39% of total marketing spend. Total UK ad/marketing spend is approximately £46bn (AA/WARC 2025).

**Working: **£46bn × 39% (offline) × 19.3% (events share of offline) = £3.5bn

*This includes all event marketing (conferences, exhibitions, experiential). Experiential as a subset is estimated at 50–70% of this, giving £1.7bn–£2.4bn.*

### **UK Market Size Summary**

| **Method** | **2026 Est. (USD)** | **2026 Est. (GBP)** | **Confidence** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Direct country (CMR) | $2.7bn | £2.1bn | Medium |
| UK share of global (BRI) | $5.8bn | £4.6bn | Low-Medium |
| Bottom-up (Gartner/IPA/WARC) | $2.2–$3.0bn | £1.7–£2.4bn | Medium |
| **Working figure used in scenarios** | **$2.7bn** | **£2.1bn** | Convergence |

Methods 1 and 3 converge around £2.0–2.4bn. The direct country estimate (£2.1bn) is used as the working figure because it sits at the intersection and comes from the only source with a UK-specific data point.

# **2. ‘Disenchanted Spend’ Evidence**

This section compiles evidence that experiential marketing spend is ‘at risk’ of being reallocated, withdrawn, or switched to a different provider.

## **2a. Client Trust and Value Perception**

**ISBA (Jan 2025): **Only 48% of ISBA members believe they get value for money from their agencies. Agency remuneration and supplier management was the top 2025 priority for ISBA’s Marketing Procurement Community. Source: ISBA Marketing Procurement Online.

**Gartner (May 2025): **39% of CMOs plan to cut agency budgets. Top actions include eliminating unproductive agency relationships, streamlining rosters, and renegotiating contracts. 22% of CMOs said GenAI has enabled them to reduce reliance on external agencies for creativity and strategy. Source: Gartner 2025 CMO Spend Survey (n=402).

**Marketing Week / B2B (2025): **15.2% of B2B firms have already cut agency spend specifically due to AI. Source: Marketing Week.

**IPA ‘Shift Happens’ (2023): **90% of UK marketers are either using or considering in-housing capabilities previously outsourced to agencies. Source: IPA.

## **2b. Measurement Dissatisfaction**

**Bizzabo (2025): **70% of event organisers reported difficulty proving ROI. Source: Bizzabo 2025 State of Events and Industry Benchmarks Report.

**Bizzabo (2026 update): **This has improved to 40% reporting difficulty in 2026, but still represents a substantial measurement gap. Source: Bizzabo 2026 Benchmarks.

**Business Research Insights (Dec 2025): **56% of marketers struggle to measure ROI effectively from experiential campaigns. Source: BRI Experiential Marketing Agency Market Report.

**ANA / Audible (2025): **Audible’s CFO described an experiential measurement category called ‘feels right’ because it is too difficult to measure rigorously. Source: ANA Brand Masters 2025.

**Experiential Marketing Measurement Coalition (Sep 2025): **Confirmed ‘there’s no broadly accepted standard metrics’ for experiential marketing. Source: eventmeasurement.org.

## **2c. Governance and Compliance Concerns**

**Bridged Media (2025): **Over 50% of organisations are unclear on what the EU AI Act requires. Source: Bridged Media.

**Bird ****&**** Bird (2025): **72–75% of leading online brands in Ireland and Germany failed EAA accessibility audits before the deadline. Source: Bird & Bird.

**EventTrack (2025): **76% of experiential marketers lack a formal sustainability policy. Source: EventTrack 2025.

No experiential agency has published a combined regulatory readiness framework addressing the overlapping compliance landscape (DUAA, EU AI Act, EAA, CSRD, DMCCA, Online Safety Act).

## **2d. Procurement Friction and Mark-Up Opacity**

**ISBA (2020/ongoing): **15% of programmatic ad spend ‘disappears’ in the supply chain — while this is digital, it has heightened procurement scrutiny across all agency relationships. Source: ISBA/PwC Programmatic Supply Chain Study.

**Gartner (2025): **Agency share of marketing budget fell to 20.7%, continuing a five-year erosion from approximately 25% in 2020. Source: Gartner 2025 CMO Spend Survey.

Experiential agencies within holding company structures (IPG, Omnicom, WPP, dentsu, Publicis) carry multi-layer commercial structures. Several agencies in the competitive intelligence pack scored 3+ on ‘procurement friction’ due to holding company overheads.

## **2e. Creative Fatigue and Experience Quality**

**Bizzabo (2024): **48% of attendees reported not feeling actively involved during events. Source: Bizzabo 2024 Event Experience Benchmark.

**Freeman (2025): **Only 15% of organisers rate their networking provisions ‘very effective’, down from prior years. 24% of potential buyers ‘missed out on the opportunity to fully evaluate, engage, or build confidence in what was offered.’ Source: Freeman 2025 Commercial Trends Report.

**CEIR (Q4 2024): **Exhibition attendance remains 17.8% below 2019 levels. Only 34.1% of individual exhibitions have surpassed their 2019 performance. Source: CEIR Total Index.

## **2f. Composite ‘At Risk’ Assessment**

**There is no single published figure for the percentage of experiential spend that is ‘disenchanted.’**

However, the evidence supports a defensible range:

**Lower bound (15%): **Represents only the proportion of B2B firms that have already cut agency spend due to AI (15.2%, Marketing Week). Conservative because it excludes all other drivers.

**Mid-range (25%): **Triangulated from: 48% of ISBA members do not believe they get value for money (so roughly half of all clients are dissatisfied); 39% of CMOs plan agency cuts (Gartner); 70% cannot prove experiential ROI (Bizzabo 2025). Assuming overlap between these groups and that dissatisfaction does not always translate to switching, 25% represents a defensible estimate of spend that is actively at risk of reallocation or provider change.

**Upper bound (35%): **Approaches the Gartner figure for planned agency budget cuts (39%) but discounts for the gap between stated intent and actual action.

# **3. Scenario Maths**

## **3a. Working Assumptions**

**Global experiential market (2026, narrow definition): **$55.5bn (BRI, Jan 2026)

**UK experiential market (2026, narrow definition): **£2.1bn / $2.7bn (Cognitive Market Research extrapolation)

## **3b. Three Scenarios**

| **Line Item** | **Low (15%)** | **Base (25%)** | **High (35%)** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **A. Global market 2026** | $55.5bn | $55.5bn | $55.5bn |
| B. Disenchanted % | 15% | 25% | 35% |
| **C. Global disenchanted (A×B)** | **$8.3bn** | **$13.9bn** | **$19.4bn** |

## **3c. Working (Line by Line)**

**Low case:** $55.5bn × 0.15 = $8.325bn global. UK: £2,100m × 0.15 = £315m.

**Base case:** $55.5bn × 0.25 = $13.875bn global. UK: £2,100m × 0.25 = £525m.

**High case:** $55.5bn × 0.35 = $19.425bn global. UK: £2,100m × 0.35 = £735m.

## **3d. UK Priority Verticals Breakdown**

Sector allocation of the UK disenchanted spend is estimated using a combination of UK marketing spend by industry (Statista/Gartner 2023: banking/finance ≈20% of UK marketing budgets; consumer/luxury ≈15%; tech ≈12%; B2B services ≈10%) and the white space analysis from the demand-side research, which identifies luxury, finance, B2B, and tech as the verticals most affected by the eight identified white spaces.

**Weighting rationale: **Finance and B2B are weighted higher because they face the strongest regulatory pressure (FCA, CSRD, EAA) and highest ROI measurement demands. Luxury is weighted for its disproportionate experiential spend per brand. Tech is weighted for digital-native brand physical experience demand.
