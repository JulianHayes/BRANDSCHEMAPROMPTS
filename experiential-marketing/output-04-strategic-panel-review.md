### b) What does “AI-native” actually mean operationally?

**Functions that should be AI-powered from day one (practically, not aspirationally):**

Proposal generation and creative concepting — using AI to produce initial creative routes, mood boards, and concept decks at speed. This is where the immediate cost advantage lives. A traditional boutique spends 40-80 hours on a pitch deck; an AI-augmented founder can cut this to 10-20 hours. That is a 4x efficiency gain on the activity that most determines whether a boutique wins or loses work.

Client intelligence and research — automated competitive monitoring, audience analysis, trend scanning. This directly supports a credible strategic positioning by demonstrating data-driven thinking from the first meeting. Cost: essentially zero with current tools. Impact: significant differentiation against boutiques who arrive at pitches with instinct rather than evidence.

Post-event reporting and measurement — AI-assisted analysis of event data, social mentions, sentiment, and content performance. The research shows 70% of event organisers cannot prove ROI (Bizzabo 2025). If Justin can deliver a post-event report in 48 hours rather than 3 weeks, with genuinely useful attribution rather than vanity metrics, that alone is a sellable capability. This requires not custom AI engineering but competent use of existing tools (Claude, GPT-4, social listening APIs, basic data integration).

Content multiplication — a single event should produce 20-50 pieces of content across formats and channels. AI makes this economically viable for a small firm. One experienced human plus AI tools can do what previously required a content team of 3-5.

**Where AI creates genuine cost advantage versus competitors:**

The real advantage is not that Justin’s AI is better. It is that he starts with no legacy. Giants have hundreds of employees doing tasks AI can now handle. They cannot restructure without mass redundancies. A new business can be built lean from day one, with AI doing the work that giants still rely on graduate hires and junior account managers for. This means Justin’s overhead structure can be fundamentally different: higher margins at lower revenue, or lower prices at similar margins.

**Where**** ****“****AI-native****”**** ****is just marketing language with no defensible moat:**

“AI-enhanced creative.” Every agency will claim this within 12 months. “AI-powered personalisation.” Meaningless without proprietary data or a proprietary model. “AI-driven insights.” Unless you own a unique dataset, you are running the same prompts as everyone else on the same foundation models. The word “native” implies structural dependence. If you can remove the AI and the business still functions the same way, you are AI-augmented, not AI-native. That is fine — AI-augmented is a genuine advantage — but do not oversell it.

### e) Procurement barriers

Let me be specific, because this is where many startups fail before they even get to demonstrate their capability:

**Professional Indemnity Insurance:** My procurement team requires a minimum of £2M PI cover, often £5M for campaigns above £250K. Many startups carry £500K or none. This is a hard stop, not a negotiable point. Justin needs to budget £3K-£8K per annum for appropriate cover before going to market.

**Track record requirements:** Most enterprise procurement processes require three comparable case studies within the last 24 months. The Amex work is strong but dates from several years ago. Justin needs current work — even pro bono or reduced-fee pilot work — to satisfy this requirement.

**Financial stability checks:** Procurement will run a credit check. A newly incorporated company with no trading history will flag as high risk. Some companies require two years of filed accounts. Workarounds include: partnering with an established firm for the first 1-2 years, offering milestone-based payment structures (so the client never has significant cash at risk), or targeting smaller divisions within large companies where procurement thresholds are lower (typically sub-£25K or sub-£50K).

**Data handling and information security:** GDPR compliance is table stakes. ISO 27001 certification is increasingly requested for any work involving customer data. Cyber Essentials (or Plus) may be required. At minimum, Justin needs a data processing agreement template, a privacy impact assessment methodology, and documented data handling procedures. For experiential work involving attendee registration, badge scanning, or post-event CRM integration, these are not optional.

**Payment terms:** Large companies pay at 60-90 days. A startup with no cash reserves and no credit facility will struggle to fund production costs that must be paid in advance. Justin needs either significant personal capital, a business credit facility, or a commercial model that requires upfront payment for production costs with strategy fees billed in arrears.

**Supplier diversity:** Increasingly, large companies have supplier diversity targets. Being a small, independent, founder-led business can actually be an advantage here if Justin registers with appropriate supplier diversity databases.

## Output 4: Honest Risks and Prerequisites

### The 3 biggest risks to this venture succeeding

**Risk 1: Founder dependency without scalable delivery.** Justin is the credential, the relationship, the creative eye, and the strategic brain. Until he hires at least one senior person and documents a repeatable methodology, the business cannot grow beyond what one person can deliver. The research shows boutiques fail at the “resource ceiling” — this business will hit it faster than most because it is selling a premium, bespoke service. **Risk 3: Market timing and awareness.** The compliance-integrated positioning depends on brands recognising that they have a compliance problem with their experiential programming. Justin may be 6-12 months early for the mass market, which means a longer runway to profitability than the revenue targets assume. Mitigation: target the most sophisticated buyers first (financial services, where regulatory awareness is highest), and invest in thought leadership (articles, conference speaking, LinkedIn) to accelerate market awareness.

### The 3 non-negotiable prerequisites before going to market

**Prerequisite 1: Professional Indemnity insurance at £2M minimum.** Without this, most enterprise buyers cannot contract with you regardless of how good you are. Budget £5K-£8K and have this in place before the first pitch.

**Prerequisite 2: One documented, named methodology.** Not a PowerPoint of principles. A step-by-step framework that describes how this company takes a brief from intake through compliance mapping, creative development, production management, and post-event measurement. Give it a proprietary name. Make it visual. This is what separates a consultancy from a freelancer in the buyer’s mind, and it is what allows a second person to deliver the work.

**Prerequisite 3: A measurement capability — human, not just tool.** Whether this is a fractional hire, a formal partnership with a measurement consultancy, or a clearly scoped arrangement with a data analyst, the business cannot go to market promising commercially accountable experiences without a credible measurement function. This does not need to be expensive (£500-£1,000 per month as a retained arrangement) but it needs to exist.
